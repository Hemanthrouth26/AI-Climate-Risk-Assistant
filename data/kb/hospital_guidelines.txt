Hospital Guidelines for Climate Risks:

Hospital Heatwave Emergency Response Protocol:
- Activate cooling centers for patients and staff.
- Ensure adequate hydration for all patients, especially elderly and infants.
- Monitor patients for heat-related illnesses and adjust treatments.
- Use backup generators if air conditioning fails.
- Train staff on heat emergency procedures.
- Stock up on electrolytes and cooling supplies.
- Coordinate with local authorities for heatwave response.
- Implement visitor restrictions during extreme heat.

Hospital Flood Emergency Preparedness Plan:
- Develop evacuation plans for patients and staff.
- Elevate critical equipment and supplies above flood levels.
- Install flood barriers and sump pumps where possible.
- Train staff on flood response and patient evacuation.
- Maintain emergency communication systems.
- Stock emergency supplies including water, food, and medications.
- Coordinate with emergency services for patient transport.
- Test backup power systems regularly.

Hospital Air Quality Management and Patient Protection:
- Install HEPA air purifiers in patient care areas.
- Monitor indoor air quality and ventilation systems.
- Use respiratory protection for staff during poor air quality.
- Limit outdoor air intake when pollution is high.
- Educate patients with respiratory conditions about air quality risks.
- Maintain emergency oxygen supplies.
- Coordinate with environmental health services.
- Implement air quality monitoring protocols.
