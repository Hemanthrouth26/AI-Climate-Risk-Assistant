Urban Guidelines for Climate Risks:

Heatwave Safety Tips for Urban Residents:
- Stay in air-conditioned buildings as much as possible.
- Use public cooling centers if home AC is unavailable.
- Create a home cooling plan with fans and shade.
- Check on neighbors, especially elderly and isolated residents.
- Avoid using ovens or other heat-generating appliances.
- Plant trees and green spaces to reduce urban heat island effect.
- Use reflective materials on roofs and pavements.
- Stay hydrated and recognize heat illness symptoms.

Urban Flood Preparedness and Evacuation Safety:
- Know flood-prone areas in your city and avoid them during rain.
- Prepare an emergency kit with essentials for evacuation.
- Elevate important documents and valuables.
- Install check valves to prevent sewer backup.
- Learn evacuation routes and have multiple exit plans.
- Avoid driving through flooded streets.
- Participate in community flood preparedness programs.
- Secure outdoor furniture and equipment.

Urban Air Pollution Health Protection Measures:
- Use public transportation or biking to reduce emissions.
- Avoid exercising outdoors during high pollution periods.
- Keep windows closed and use air conditioning with filters.
- Plant urban gardens and support green initiatives.
- Monitor air quality apps and plan activities accordingly.
- Wear masks during high pollution days.
- Support policies for cleaner air and reduced emissions.
- Educate about indoor air quality improvement.
