Student Guidelines for Climate Risks:

Student Safety Guidelines During Heatwaves:
- Stay hydrated and drink water regularly throughout the day.
- Wear light clothing, hats, and sunscreen when outdoors.
- Take breaks in shaded or air-conditioned areas during outdoor activities.
- Recognize signs of heat exhaustion: dizziness, nausea, heavy sweating.
- Avoid physical exertion during peak heat hours.
- Use fans or cooling devices in study areas.
- Parents and teachers should monitor students closely.
- Learn emergency response for heat-related illnesses.

Student Safety During Floods and Heavy Rainfall:
- Stay away from flooded areas and fast-moving water.
- If caught in flood, move to higher ground immediately.
- Do not walk, swim, or drive through floodwaters.
- Have an emergency kit with water, snacks, and first aid supplies.
- Know school evacuation routes and emergency procedures.
- Avoid playing near drains or low-lying areas during rain.
- Listen to weather alerts and follow school instructions.

Student Health Protection During Air Pollution:
- Stay indoors when air quality is poor and keep windows closed.
- Use air purifiers in classrooms and homes.
- Wear masks when outdoors on high pollution days.
- Limit outdoor recess and physical education during poor air quality.
- Learn about respiratory health and pollution effects.
- Wash hands and face after outdoor exposure.
- Parents should check air quality forecasts before school activities.
